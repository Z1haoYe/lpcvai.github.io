2022 Low Power Computer Vision Challenge Overview

Since 2015, the IEEE Low Power Computer Vision Challenges have attracted top researchers worldwide showing their solutions that can achieve high accuracy with low energy consumption.

Computer Vision research has given little consideration to speed or computation time, and even less to constraints such as power/energy, memory footprint and model size.

Nevertheless, addressing all of these metrics is essential if advances in Computer Vision are going to be widely available on mobile and AR/VR devices.

This workshop focused on efficient deep learning algorithms, models, and systems for computer vision.

2022 Unmanned Aerial Vehicle(UAV) Chase Challenge Overview

In the 2022 UAV Chase Challenge, competitors used computer vision and machine learning to develop solutions for object tracking. Complete solutions included not only an object detection model, but also a complete control system to pilot a drone. Qualifying solutions were tested on an actual drone in Purdue's Unmanned Aerial Systems test facility.

More information can be found on the ri4rover website.
